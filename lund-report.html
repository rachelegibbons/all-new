Lund Report Refresh

UI: Wireframing, Visual Design

The Lund Report is the first independent news site in Oregon dedicated to educating people about the inner workings of the health care industry.

In 2017 the Lund Report underwent a brand refresh. To compliment the work of their brand agency Metal Toad reflected the new look and feel across Lund Report's web presence. With a small budget, we took an efficient approach — keeping the bones of the website, we cleaned it up to be more legible and added a small handful of new features.

The process included rapid hand wireframes, presented to the client for approval — the process was lean and scrappy. Next the selected wireframes were cleaned digitally to make sure all stakeholders were aligned before moving into visual design. Finally, the visual design was applied on top of the wires.

See the before and after below.

Metal Toad Logo Before & After

The Metal Toad brandmark remained the same. The logotype, on the other hand, had been replaced. The previous slab serif logotype was difficult to read when scaled down to smaller sizes and its industrial feel was better suited for a younger, scrappier Metal Toad.

The new logotype, Gotham, was chosen because it is highly legible at small sizes, making it ideal for logotype. Gotham was inspired by an engineer’s idea of “basic lettering.” Tobias Frere-Jones, the typeface’s designer is quoted as saying, “It’s the kind of letter an engineer would make.” due to its geometry and mathematical proportions. This modern logotype is a nod to Metal Toad’s engineering focus and builds upon the core value of user experience.
